📚 Documentation

Getting Started

The Stock Market Simulator lets you practise trading strategies without risking real money. Configure your simulation parameters, watch markets move in real time, and use the Auto-Trading Bot to automate strategies.

  1. Fill in the Simulation Setup fields and click Start Simulation.
  2. Press ▶ Play to run the simulation, or use Step → to advance one day at a time.
  3. Watch the chart and portfolio update as each day passes.
  4. Click ↺ Reset at any time to return to the setup screen.

Simulation Setup

OptionDescription
Starting CapitalThe cash balance you begin with (default $10,000).
Simulation PeriodNumber of trading days to simulate (30 days – 5 years).
Market ConditionSets the overall trend: Bull (up), Neutral (random), Bear (down), or High Volatility.
Number of StocksHow many stocks to include (3, 5, or 8).
Trading CostFlat fee deducted from each buy or sell order (default $1.00).

Chart & Controls

  • Click a stock tab above the chart to switch between stocks.
  • Hover over the chart to see the exact price and any trades on that day.
  • Green triangles (▲) mark buy events; red triangles (▼) mark sell events.
  • Use the Speed slider to control simulation pace (1× – 10×).

Portfolio

  • Cash – Your available buying power.
  • Holdings Value – Current market value of all open positions.
  • Total Value – Cash + Holdings Value.
  • P&L – Profit or loss relative to your starting capital.

Auto-Trading Bot

Write a JavaScript snippet that runs automatically each simulation day. Enable or disable the bot with the Enable checkbox. Select a predefined strategy from the dropdown, or write your own.

Bot API Reference

NameTypeDescription
pricesObjectMap of { symbol: currentPrice } for all stocks.
holdingsObjectMap of { symbol: { shares, avgCost } } for open positions.
cashNumberAvailable cash at the start of the current day.
dayNumberCurrent simulation day (1-based).
totalDaysNumberTotal number of days in the simulation.
buy(symbol, shares)FunctionBuy shares of symbol at today's price.
sell(symbol, shares)FunctionSell shares of symbol at today's price.
priceHistory(symbol, n)FunctionReturns the last n closing prices (oldest first).

Built-in Strategies

  • SMA (Simple Moving Average) – Buys when price dips 3% below its 10-day average; sells when it rises 5% above.
  • RSI – Buys when RSI < 30 (oversold); sells when RSI > 70 (overbought).
  • Momentum – Buys top-performing stocks over 20 days; sells the worst performers.
  • Buy & Hold – Invests equally across all stocks on day 1 and never sells.
  • Mean Reversion – Buys when a stock falls 5%+ below its 30-day average; sells on an 8%+ recovery.
